Capture the Moment: Documentation Team's Cameras

Who We Are

Base Camp Coding Academy is a school based in Water Valley, Mississippi that offers a year-long tuition-free program to high school graduates. Upon acceptance into the program, students are taught computer programming, regardless of experience in a hands-on setting, which includes:

1. Front-End Development

Front-end developers use languages such as HTML and CSS to create the things a user sees while going through a website, for example.

2. Back-End Development

Back-end developers use languages such as Python to create several different processes, which users are unable to see.

3. Professionalism

You read that correctly! Along with programming, Base Camp aims to teach their students personal skills they will need to land a job, including interviewing, leadership, and effective communication.

The Role of Social Media

In our current age, a strong social media presence is paramount to being successful. Along with their websites, schools often use at least one social media page, if not more, to increase relations and communication with parents and students. Due to the influx of Twitter posts, which can reach up to 500 million daily, schools sometimes compete when appearing in a feed, or even being read when appearing. To combat this, posts will often include at least one photo, which can be used to convey an emotion or idea, but is also important in increasing user interaction. It's shown that posts will gain 94% more views if a video or photo is included, which should be no surprise considering 64% of the population relies on visual learning.

Why We Need Cameras

Currently, the Documentation Team, a group of students that manages all of Base Camp's social media accounts, attempts to put a post out at least twice a week, though the team is hoping to take on more challenges, such as writing a newsletter. While two of the students own phones with impressive camera capabilities, they aren't always available for photography, causing some posts to contain lackluster images. We believe that the addition of cameras will improve the quality of all photos taken in the future.

Our Goal

The purpose of this fundraiser is to allow the school to purchase at least two cameras which are to be dedicated for school use, not only for this year's current class, but any others that come later. These cameras will ensure high quality images at every event, even if the designated photographers are unavailable. By contributing to this fundraiser, you will be enabling a group of students to increase the school's visibility so that more high schoolers will hear about our opportunity.
